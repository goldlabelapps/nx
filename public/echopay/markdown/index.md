Open banking is still in its early years, but its growth is clear. However, there are still plenty of misconceptions about open banking and one of those is its impact on your credit score.

#### Future proofed payment

Ultimately, open banking does not have a direct impact on your credit score. This is because open banking allows you to securely share your data with third-party suppliers, like lenders and financial service providers. 

According to GoCardless, open banking can help reduce the stress associated with creditworthiness, as it keeps the process private, speeds up the application , and offers an improved customer experience. 

The report said: “Open banking credit scoring solutions provide lenders with valuable data that allows them to make better decisions, levelling the competitive playing field.
