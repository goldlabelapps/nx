Terpenes (what they are + why people rate them)
Terpenes are aromatic compounds that shape smell, taste, and the “feel” for many users. Exact terp percentages vary by batch, but Insane OG is commonly associated with a classic OG backbone:

Myrcene — earthy, kushy depth. Often associated by consumers with heavier body-led unwind as dosage climbs. Research interest includes calming/anti-inflammatory pathways (not a medical promise).
Limonene — brighter lift. Often linked (by consumers) with a cleaner, happier edge that keeps the headspace from feeling muddy. Studied in early mood/stress pathway research.
β-Caryophyllene — peppery, woody spice. Famous for that “black pepper OG” snap; notable in research because it interacts with CB2 receptors and is studied for inflammation-related pathways (not a guarantee).
Important: terpene science is still developing and responses vary person-to-person. This is a flavour/experience map — not medical advice.
