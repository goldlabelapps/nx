Every Full Spectrum Clouds flavour has its own terpene stack. Here’s a quick guide to the main names you’ll see so you don’t have to guess what they are:

Myrcene — earthy, musky, sometimes mango-like. Common in cannabis, hops and lemongrass. Often sits behind “heavy” fruity and kushy aromas, adding depth and that slightly dank, herbal body.
Limonene — sharp citrus peel (orange, lemon, lime). Found in citrus rinds and many “clean” smelling strains. Brings bright, zesty top notes that keep fruit profiles feeling fresh instead of sickly.
β-Caryophyllene — black pepper, clove and warm wood. Present in pepper, cloves and lots of kush/dessert strains. Adds a spicy backbone and “grown up” edge under sweeter flavours.
α/β-Pinene — fresh pine needles and rosemary. The classic evergreen note in many “OG” style profiles, giving crisp woodland and herbal lift over fruit.
Terpinolene — bright, slightly floral, with mixed fruit and herb notes. Common in Strawberry Cough-type lines; can make blends feel airy and “breezy” rather than heavy.
Linalool — lavender, spring flowers, soft spice. Found in lavender and some dessert-leaning strains; often read as “calm, bath-product floral” on the nose.
Ocimene — sweet, woody, herbal with citrus hints. Shows up in basil, mint and some candy/fruit cultivars, helping with that juicy, almost tropical top note.
Humulene — earthy, woody, hoppy. Big in hops and some classic “beer-ish” cannabis aromas; adds that savoury, grown, slightly bitter twist that keeps things from being pure sugar.
Bisabolol — soft chamomile, honey, floral. The main aroma in chamomile; in cannabis it adds a soothing, gentle perfume in the background of some dessert and apple/banana profiles.
Farnesene — crisp green apple, woody, light citrus. Found in apples, hops and some green-leaning strains; it’s the “bite into an apple peel” note you sometimes get on the nose.
