title: Glossary
---

**Access routes**: The ways patients can legally obtain medical cannabis, such as prescriptions or clinics.

**Cannabinoids**: Chemical compounds found in cannabis, including THC and CBD, that interact with the body’s endocannabinoid system.

**Clinics**: Medical facilities or services where patients can receive consultations and prescriptions for medical cannabis.

**Compliance**: Adhering to laws and regulations governing the use, prescription, and supply of medical cannabis.

**Hybrids**: Cannabis strains bred from both sativa and indica plants, combining characteristics of each.

**Prescriptions**: Authorizations from a qualified healthcare professional allowing legal access to medical cannabis.

**Risks**: Potential negative effects or legal consequences associated with cannabis use.

**Sativa/Indica**: Two primary types of cannabis plants, each with distinct effects and characteristics.

**Terpenes**: Aromatic compounds in cannabis that contribute to its smell, flavor, and potential effects.

**UK law**: The legal framework in the United Kingdom governing the use, possession, and prescription of cannabis.

**CBPM**: Cannabis-Based Product for Medicinal use; a legal term for regulated medical cannabis products in the UK.

**Edibles**: Cannabis-infused food products, such as gummies or baked goods, used as an alternative to smoking or vaping.

**Flower**: The dried bud of the cannabis plant, typically used for smoking or vaporizing.

**Legal**: Relating to the laws and regulations that govern medical cannabis in the UK.

**Licensed pharmacies**: Pharmacies authorized to dispense medical cannabis products under UK law.

**Medical cannabis**: Cannabis prescribed by a healthcare professional for therapeutic purposes.

**UK**: United Kingdom; refers to the region and its specific laws and regulations regarding medical cannabis.

**Vape**: A method of consuming cannabis by inhaling vaporized cannabinoids, usually from a vaporizer device.
